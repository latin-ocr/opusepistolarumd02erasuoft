Domenico Grimani (19 Feb. 1461—27 Aug. 1523) was the son of Antonio
Grimani, who from being an exiled general of the Republic became Doge in 1521.
Domenico, who was created Cardinal in 1493, and Pati-iarah of Aquileia 1498,
shared his father’s exile at Rome, and rose to be Cardinal Bp. of Porto, second
only to Raffaelle Riario. He was a patron of literature, and his rich collections
of MSS. now form part of the Library of St. Mark's at Venice. Erasmus
addressed to him his Paraphrase on St. Paul's Epistle to the Romans, Louvain,
Th. Martens, Nov. 1517. See Ciaconius iii. 180, 1 and NBG.]
