Sylvester Gigli (1463--18 Apr. 1521) of Lucca had the good fortune to succeed
his uncle as Bp. of Worcester and English agent at the Papal court in 1498.
In the autumn of 1504 he was sent to England on a Papal mission (see Ep. 218
introd.). and remained about the Court until the spring of 1512, when he
returned to Rome as one of the English ambassadors to the Lateran Council.
As a colleague and rival, he was accused of complicity in Bainbridge’s death
in 1514, but was acquitted. He was helpful to Wolsey in obtaining the
cardinalate, and was thenceforward Wolsey’s chief agent in Rome3 but he
failed to secure the cardinalate for himself. See Creighton, Historical Essays,
pp. 202-20‚ and in DNB.]
